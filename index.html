Dorian Gray's Metaphor Tracker

What is a metaphor tracker?

This project uses XML to create a website capable of detecting and analyzing metaphors and similes in The Picture of Dorian Gray by Oscar Wilde. The goal is to compile figurative language results and compare similarities between them — identifying recurring words, shared imagery, and patterns. This allows for clearer visualization of Wilde’s image system and stylistic tendencies.

Discourse about the Novel

The Picture of Dorian Gray by Oscar Wilde is commonly cited as an exploration of the consequences of vanity, aestheticism, and moral corruption.

"The only way to get rid of temptation is to yield to it."

Commonly Stated Themes

  • Beauty and Youth
  • The Corruption of the Soul
  • The Influence of Lord Henry
  • Art vs. Reality
  • Morality and Consequence

Interpretation & Analysis

Much of the novel blurs the line between reality and art, aesthetic and truth, thus it feels appropriate to try and understand Wilde’s novel through his use of metaphor — what common images he is conjuring, and which lines he tends to blur.

About This Website

This website hopes to cultivate thoughtful literary interpretation, close reading, and thematic analysis of Wilde's novel.
